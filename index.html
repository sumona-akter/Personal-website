About Me

I am a dedicated Microbiologist with over three years of experience specializing in clinical microbiology, molecular diagnostics, and public health genomics. My professional journey has involved processing clinical samples, conducting advanced molecular diagnostics, and performing antimicrobial resistance testing. I have a strong passion for research in public health and have contributed to projects addressing neonatal Candidemia, bacterial disease surveillance, and microbial resistome analysis.

I hold an MSc and BSc in Microbiology from Jagannath University, where I conducted research on hydrocarbon-degrading bacteria. This research highlights the potential of bioremediation as an eco-friendly solution for environmental pollution.

My technical expertise includes advanced techniques like PCR, ELISA, Sanger sequencing, next-generation sequencing, and bioinformatics tools such as Python and R for data analysis. Alongside my research, I have teaching experience as a graduate laboratory assistant and a part-time lecturer, fostering critical thinking among students.
